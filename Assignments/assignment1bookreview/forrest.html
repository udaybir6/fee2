Forrest Gump

by Winston Groom

Ratings:★★★☆☆

Genre:Novel, Drama, Humor, Bildungsroman

Forrest Gump is a book about a man with a low IQ who goes on many adventures throughout his life. The story is told from Forrest's perspective as he recounts his experiences, including his time in the Vietnam War, his success as a college football player, and his encounters with historical figures like John F. Kennedy and Elvis Presley. The book also explores Forrest's relationships with his childhood friend Jenny and his mother, as well as his journey to find meaning and purpose in his life.

The book is filled with humor, heartwarming moments, and thought-provoking themes. It delves into the idea of destiny and the impact of chance on one's life, as well as the importance of love, friendship, and perseverance. Through Forrest's unique perspective, readers are taken on a journey that is both entertaining and touching, and they are left with a deeper understanding of the complexities of human experience. Overall, Forrest Gump is a compelling and unforgettable story that will resonate with readers of all ages.
